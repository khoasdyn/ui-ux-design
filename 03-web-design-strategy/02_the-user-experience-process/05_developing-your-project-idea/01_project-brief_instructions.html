As mentioned earlier, in this course and the one that follows, Web Design: Wireframes and Prototypes , you will be working on a large-scale project in which you will be designing a plan for a responsive website. Each week you will complete development milestones (in the form of short assignments) that you will add to a PDF document, which you will share with your peers for review.

Here is the premise of the assignment:

Imagine that you've been hired by an entrepreneur who wants to open a new, delivery-only establishment that delivers food (or drinks) to customers living nearby. You have been asked to plan and design the restaurant’s website. The centerpiece of this website will be an online food customization and ordering system.

Before you can start, you must determine a few parameters for the restaurant business. You have to decide on a very specific, customizable food product, a primary target audience, the restaurant’s price range, and its name. So, in a way, imagine that you are the restaurateur’s business partner or their business consultant. Using this lens will allow you to approach the project in a very real-life manner.

It might be helpful to come up with a few ideas (maybe 3) and then pick the one that you are most excited about. Here are two tips for coming up with a successful idea for this project:

  • Your project idea should be specific and not too broad

  • You should be really excited about your project idea
