Welcome back everyone. This week, you'll continue the visual design of your websites. Having worked on the homepage mark-up last week, it's time to design the remaining screens. As an important side, I will take you on a short expedition to the land of codes. Although this specialization isn't about learning how to code, I want to at least, introduce you to the technologies that make the web work. We'll look at a few coding examples. After all the web is built upon certain languages, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and in order to be an effective UX designer, it is helpful to have a rudimentary understanding of these technologies. In a perfect world, you might go on and expand your skills as a front-end developer, so that you can code your own designs and launch your own websites. At the very least, however, you should be able to effectively communicate with the developer. And knowing just a little bit of code will go a long way for this communication to be successful.
