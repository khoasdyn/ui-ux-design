UX Design Fundamentals is the second course in the UI/UX Design Specialization. To read more about the other courses in this Specialization please visit https://www.coursera.org/specializations/ui-ux-design.

This course builds on the knowledge and skills in the first course in the Specialization, Visual Elements in User Interface Design . I recommend you complete that course first prior to starting this one.

This course is designed for a beginner, so anyone with some visual or graphic design experience but no specific experience in UI or UX Design will fit right in. The concepts presented in this course are foundational to user experience design, and you'll have an opportunity to build on your skills in the subsequent courses in the Specialization.

About the Assignments

There are four required Graded Assignments in this course, plus six Peer Review Assignments . While the Peer Review Assignments are not mandatory, it is highly encouraged that you complete these assignments to gain the full benefit of this course.

You will need to achieve a minimum grade of 75% in each of the weekly Graded Assignments to pass the course. For your final grade, Graded Assignments are weighted as follows.

  • Week 1 Graded Assignment #1: Ideation, Articulation, and Development (20% of final grade)

  • Week 2 Graded Assignment #2: Planning, Testing, Researching, and Mapping (25% of final grade)

  • Week 3 Graded Assignment #3: What Goes Where (25% of final grade)

  • Week 4 Graded Assignment #4: Making It By Faking It (30% of final grade)

For each Peer Review Assignment you are expected to complete a review of at least 2 of your peers . Peer Review Assignments also help build your skill at design critique.

Throughout the course you will find short, optional activities which I encourage you to complete. These include readings, discussion prompts , and "Try it Yourself" exercises, meant to be completed "off-platform". These are not required, but completing all of these activities will help you complete your assignment at the end of the week.

Required Tools

To complete the design assignments you will need access to a desktop or laptop computer with the appropriate software installed (see below). To succeed in graphic design, especially in designing for screens, you really need to know your way around a computer. And you can't really do user interface design work properly on a smartphone or tablet.

While it’s not required, we strongly recommend that you also have access to a smartphone to test out your designs and the designs of your peers, as your final deliverable in this course will be a prototype of a mobile app.

In Week 2, we recommend Adobe Illustrator or InDesign for creating your sitemaps as those are the programs that we frequently use.

In Week 3, you'll start building your wireframes. You can continue to use Adobe Illustrator and InDesign for this, or use a prototyping software specifically for this purpose, such as Figma.

In Week 4, you will be asked to create a clickable prototype from your wireframes. This will be demonstrated using Figma , but we also recommend Marvel as an alternative.

In summary, you’re welcome to use any program of your choice in this course as long as you're able to accomplish the following tasks:

  1. Export your sitemap and wireframe designs to PDF in order to complete assignments from Week 2 and Week 3.

  2. Provide a publicly available URL to a working prototype in order to complete the assignment in Week 4.

Work completed by hand is not acceptable for the Peer Reviewed design assignments in this course.
