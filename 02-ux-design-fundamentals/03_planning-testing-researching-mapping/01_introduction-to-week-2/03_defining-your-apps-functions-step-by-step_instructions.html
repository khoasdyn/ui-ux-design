Last week, you came up with four possible functions for your app. By the end of this week, you will illustrate at least two of these functions in a sitemap of your app.

But before you begin, it is useful to ideate how your users will interact with your app's content by writing out the user pathway in a step-by-step process. If this process sounds a bit familiar, it might be because in our last course, Visual Elements of User Interface Design, you wrote a description of how your users would interact with the app you created in that course.

However, it is important to distinguish that in this case, you won't be describing how users will interact with your app, but what tasks the user needs to do in order to complete the function. Let's refer my GetPet app as an example.

Function: Search for lost pets

  1. Identify pet search criteria, such as species/breed, name, and/or description.

  2. Review pet search results.

  3. If your pet is among the returned results, claim your pet.

  4. Connect with the person who found your pet.

Try it yourself with your own app idea and write down the steps on a piece of paper. Try to avoid describing the tasks as interactions with the interface, such “press the search button to get a result" or "open the menu."
