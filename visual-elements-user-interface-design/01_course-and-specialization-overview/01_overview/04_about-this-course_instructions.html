Visual Elements of User Interface Design is the first course in the UI/UX Design Specialization. To read more about the other courses in this Specialization please visit https://www.coursera.org/specializations/ui-ux-design .

All of the courses in the UI/UX Design Specialization are taught from a graphic design perspective, so it's ideal to have some ability in visual form-making or visual communication before you begin. Fundamentals of Graphic Design , the first course in the Graphic Design Specialization, also offered by CalArts on Coursera, would be an excellent course to take in combination with or as a prerequisite to this Specialization.

This course is designed for a beginner, so anyone with some visual or graphic design experience but no specific experience in UI or UX Design will fit right in. The concepts presented in this course are foundational to user interface design, and you'll have an opportunity to build on your skills in the subsequent courses in the Specialization. For slightly more advanced learners, the concepts presented in this course will be more of a review, but you'll have plenty of opportunity to experiment and specialize within the assigned project.

About the assignments

There are four required Graded Assignments in this course and three additional Peer Review Assignments . While the Peer Review Assignments are not mandatory, it is highly encouraged that you complete these assignments to gain the full benefit of this course.

You will need to achieve a minimum grade of 75% in each of the weekly Graded Assignments to pass the course. For your final grade, Graded Assignments are weighted as follows.

  • Week 1 Graded Assignment #1: User Interface Design Foundations (20% of final grade)

  • Week 2 Graded Assignment #2: Formal Elements of UI Design (25% of final grade)

  • Week 3 Graded Assignment #3: Active UI Design Elements (25% of final grade)

  • Week 4 Graded Assignment #4: Composing UI Design (30% of final grade)

As you complete each Peer Review Assignment you are expected to review at least 2 of your peers , for a total of 6 peer reviews minimum by the end of the course. You may, of course, review more than 6 peer assignments.

Throughout the course you will find short, optional activities which I encourage you to complete. These include readings, discussion prompts, and "Try it Yourself" exercises, meant to be completed "off-platform". These are not required, but completing all of these activities will help you complete your assignment at the end of the week.

Required Tools

To complete the design assignments you will need access to a desktop or laptop computer with the appropriate software installed (see below). To succeed in graphic design, especially in designing for screens, you really need to know your way around a computer. And you can't really do graphic design work properly on a smartphone or tablet.

In Week 2, I recommend Adobe Photoshop , Illustrator or InDesign for creating your mood board as you’ll have more flexibility in arranging images into a composition on a single page. For that reason, I don’t recommend typical mood board services such as Pinterest for this assignment. Your mood board needs to be exported to PDF.

In Weeks 3 and 4, for drawing your interface elements and assembling your interface sketches you may use any vector-based drawing program, such as Illustrator or InDesign .

Work completed by hand is not acceptable for the graded assignments in this course.
