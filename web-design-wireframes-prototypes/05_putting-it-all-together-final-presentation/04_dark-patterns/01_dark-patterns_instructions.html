You may have heard about dark patterns: user interfaces created specifically to trick users into doing something they probably didn’t intend to. They’re called dark because they are a little, well, shady—they don’t keep the user’s best interests in mind. This week’s readings should inspire you to contemplate the designer’s role in creating ethical products.
