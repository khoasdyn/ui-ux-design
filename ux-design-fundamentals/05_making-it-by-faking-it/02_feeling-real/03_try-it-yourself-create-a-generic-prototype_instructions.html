Try it yourself: Create a generic prototype

At the end of this week, you're going to submit a link to a working, clickable prototype of your app. But before you do so, I encourage you to become comfortable with the prototyping software first by creating a generic prototype.

In Marvel , you can design a generic prototype using the software's palette of UI elements.

In Figma , you can design a generic prototype using components from one of a variety of free UI kits .

Try creating any of the following: a simple camera app, a note-taking app, or an app to track how much water you have consumed in a day. Alternatively, examine an app you already have on your phone and try creating a variation of it.

Once you're comfortable with using the program, return to watching the rest of this week's videos to learn more specific strategies related to creating your own app prototype.
